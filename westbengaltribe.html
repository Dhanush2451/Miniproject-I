The Chakma Tribe: A Deeper Dive

Chakma oral history says they migrated to Arakan from the ancient kingdom of Magadha in the present Bihar state of India. The Chakma believe they are part of Buddha's Sakya clan of the Himalayan tribes. They gradually migrated to Arakan and extended their territory to the nearby hills of the Chittagong Hill Tracts. The Arakanese people referred to the Chakmas as Saks, Theks, or Thaikhs. In 1546 CE, while the Arakanese king Min Bin was fighting a battle with the Burmese, the Sak king attacked Northern Arakan Roma and occupied the Arakanese-controlled Chacomas of the Northern Arakan Mountains.

Origins and Distribution:

Significant Chakma populations are found in the northeast Indian states of Arunachal Pradesh, Tripura, Assam,Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha.Subdivided into various subgroups, each with distinct customs, dialects, and traditions.

The Chakma tribe, primarily residing in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, Bangladesh, sustains their livelihood through agriculture, handicrafts, fishing, and livestock rearing. However, they face persistent challenges such as land rights disputes, socio-economic marginalization, and struggles in preserving their cultural identity amidst modernization. Limited access to education and healthcare further exacerbates their socio-economic plight. Additionally, environmental degradation poses a threat to their traditional livelihoods, particularly in regions where shifting cultivation is practiced. Addressing these challenges requires concerted efforts to secure land rights, promote cultural preservation, improve access to essential services, and foster sustainable development initiatives.

  • Chakma paintings are known for their vivid colors and expressive style, often used to depict stories, myths, and legends passed down through generations..
  • Paints were made from natural elements like charcoal, clay, and vegetable dyes, applied on cloth or handmade paper.
  • Paintings often served religious purposes, adorning altars or depicting scenes from Buddhist scriptures and folklore.
  • Geometric patterns and symbols play a crucial role, representing elements like the sun, moon, mountains, and rivers, holding cultural significance.

The Chakma's face numerous challenges that impact their social, economic, and cultural well-being. These challenges include land rights issues and displacement, leading to the disruption of their traditional way of life and livelihoods.

  1. Displacement and Land Dispossession:

    2. Construction of the Kaptai Dam in Bangladesh displaced thousands of Chakmas from ancestral lands. Development projects continue to threaten their land and livelihood. In India, some Chakmas remain classified as refugees, impacting their rights and access to resources.

  2. Human Rights Abuses:

    3. Reports of arbitrary arrests, torture, and extrajudicial killings by security forces have been documented. Restrictions on freedom of movement and expression are often placed on Chakma communities.

  3. Discrimination and Marginalization:

    4. The Chakmas often face discrimination based on their ethnicity and religious beliefs. Political tensions and conflicts further marginalize their communities.
